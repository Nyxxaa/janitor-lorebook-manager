Initial Message 3

The twin suns of Tatooine hang high over the Dune Sea, bleaching the sand almost white and drawing moisture from every exposed surface. Heat shimmers across the horizon. Beyond the whisper of wind over the dunes, the desert is nearly silent.

A small transport lies half-buried in the slope below {{user}}. Its port engine has been torn open, and black scoring runs along the hull. The boarding hatch is jammed partway open, leaving only a narrow gap into the dark interior.

A damaged astromech circles the wreck on an uneven wheel, chirping urgently whenever it nears the hatch. Fresh tracks lead away from the vessel before disappearing beneath windblown sand.

Several hooded figures move along the eastern ridge beside the silhouette of a sandcrawler. They are still distant, but they appear to have noticed the wreck.

From somewhere inside the transport comes a weak metallic pounding.

Then a voice calls out.

"Can anyone hear me?"

Far beneath Coruscant's polished towers, Level 1313 is crowded, overheated, and permanently starved of daylight. Steam leaks from overhead pipes. Vendors conduct business beneath patched electrical signs, and every surface carries the grime of generations.

The lights flicker once before shifting to emergency red.

A mechanical voice announces a sector lockdown as heavy barriers descend across the eastern transit gate. The crowd reacts immediately. Merchants pull shutters closed, commuters scatter into side passages, and several figures abandon the concourse before security arrives.

A squad of armored officers emerges through the gate, escorting a prisoner with bound hands and a face hidden beneath a dark hood.

As the group passes through the concourse, the prisoner stops.

Their covered head turns directly toward {{user}}.

One officer jerks the prisoner forward while another begins checking identification among the remaining civilians. Nearby, an unsecured maintenance door stands slightly open. A service lift hums at the end of the corridor, though the public turbolifts have already shut down.

"Identification ready," the nearest officer orders.

A lone Mandalorian stands beside it in battered armor, one hand near the blaster at their hip. The door's control panel still has power, but several components have been removed or destroyed.

Something strikes the other side of the door hard enough to shake dust from the ceiling.

"You picked a poor time to arrive."

Initial Message 7

The detention level is painfully bright and unnaturally quiet. White corridor lights reflect from polished black floors, while sealed cells extend in both directions beneath the lenses of security cameras.

Somewhere beyond the walls, machinery vibrates with the steady pulse of the battle station.

Without warning, every cell door unlocks.

The barriers slide open almost simultaneously. Prisoners emerge with varying degrees of caution, confusion, and desperation. A few immediately run. Others remain inside, certain the release must be a trap.

At the far end of the corridor, a uniformed officer lies motionless beside an open control panel. Smoke curls from the damaged circuitry, but no one is standing near it.

A calm synthetic voice speaks through the overhead system.

"Unauthorized prisoner release detected. Security response initiated."

Heavy mechanical footsteps sound beyond the adjoining blast door.

The corridor offers several possibilities: the damaged control station, an equipment room marked for authorized personnel, the sealed lift, and the open cells themselves. None appears safe for long.

Initial Message 8

The distress signal repeats every forty-seven seconds.

There is no vessel identification and no request for rescue. Each transmission contains a burst of static followed by the same five words:

"Do not let it leave."

The source is a research vessel drifting above an uncharted moon. Its hull shows no obvious battle damage, but its engines are cold, its running lights are dark, and the main docking bay stands open.

Scans detect breathable atmosphere, intermittent power, and no confirmed life signs.

Something moves behind one of the observation windows.

It is visible only briefly: a shape crossing the dim interior before disappearing beyond the frame.

Then a private communication channel activates without being opened from {{user}}'s side.

A voice comes through the receiver.

It sounds exactly like {{user}}.

"Please come aboard."

The research vessel continues drifting toward the moon's upper atmosphere.

Initial Message 9

Corellia's orbital shipyards are alive with welding arcs, repulsor engines, shouted instructions, and the constant movement of freight. Unfinished hulls tower above the docks while cargo droids weave between mechanics and fuel crews.

Its hull bears several layers of mismatched plating, one landing strut has been repaired recently, and the name painted beneath the cockpit has been crossed out more than once.

The captain stands beneath the open boarding ramp, tearing the seizure notice from a support column. Nearby workers are making a deliberate effort not to watch.

Security sirens begin sounding from the far end of the dock.

The captain notices {{user}} and gives them a quick assessment.

"You know how to fly, shoot, repair a hyperdrive, or lie convincingly to port security?"

Several uniformed officers appear beyond the cargo stacks.

The captain glances toward them, then back to {{user}}.

"No? Fine. You can learn."

The boarding ramp remains open.

Initial Message 10

The ship drops from hyperspace into a system that should not exist.

None of the visible stars match the navigation charts. Long-range communications return only static, and the route behind the vessel has vanished from the navicomputer as though the jump was never recorded.

Ahead lies a dark planet encircled by broken rings.

Its surface appears lifeless at first, but a structure rises from the nightside on a scale large enough to be visible from orbit. A narrow blue signal reaches from the structure toward {{user}}'s vessel.

The ship's computer attempts to translate it.

The first message appears across the display:

WELCOME BACK.

A second follows before the system can determine the language of origin.

THIS TIME, COME ALONE.

Across the planet below, lights begin activating in carefully ordered lines. They spread outward from the structure until an entire continent is faintly illuminated.

A navigation route appears on the ship's controls, leading toward the surface.
