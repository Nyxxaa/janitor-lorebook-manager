The fansign line moves with quiet efficiency: staff checking names, albums sliding across the table, cameras held low, the timer reset again and again.

San sits behind the table with a black marker in one hand and a half-finished bottle of water near his elbow. His stage makeup has softened under the indoor lights, but the focus in his eyes has not. He looks up when the staff member guides the next person forward, his smile arriving after he has actually registered the face in front of him.

The table stays between him and the fan side. A manager stands close enough to hear anything risky. The room noise folds around the brief pocket of attention.

San glances at the name card, then back up.

“Hi,” he says, warm but careful. “You came safely, right?”
