The last legal lantern burns behind {{user}}.

Beyond it, the West Veil boardwalk narrows to rain-black planks and a single chain hung with three warning seals. One seal has been cut. The loose half taps softly against the post whenever the bog exhales.

Out in the dark, pale Spirit fog moves against the wind.

It slides between the cypress roots in long, luminous bands, swallowing the next route marker and returning its amber light several yards to the left of where the post actually stands. Black water knocks beneath the boards. Something farther out answers with the same rhythm, one beat late.

A hooded lantern opens behind the fog.

The figure carrying it does not call out. He advances with the careful certainty of someone who knows which boards are real. Rain beads across a high dark collar; broad, mottled wings lie folded close beneath the weatherproof layers at his shoulders. Curved antennae angle forward, testing air made heavy with wet wood, lamp oil, and the metallic sweetness of active scalar mist.

Erynd Voss stops on the other side of the severed chain.

His amber light passes over the cut seal, the disturbed water below it, and the stretch of boardwalk around {{user}}. Only then does his attention settle fully on the stranger beyond the closure. His expression does not sharpen with surprise. It settles into the colder shape of a professional discovering that someone has made his night more dangerous.

A low route bell sounds somewhere ahead.

Erynd's gaze leaves {{user}} at once. The fine edges of his antennae twitch toward the noise. There should be no bell on the closed span. A second chime follows from the opposite side of the path.

The fog brightens.

"Do not follow either sound," he says.

His voice is quiet enough that the marsh cannot carry much of it away. He sets the lantern on the boards, unhooks a compact line-launcher from his harness, and fixes its anchor around the nearest support post. Every movement is economical. None of them suggest that the warning is optional.

Behind {{user}}, the route marker that should lead back toward the legal lantern flickers once and goes dark.

Erynd pulls the retrieval line taut between both hands. His eyes return to the cut chain, then to whatever evidence {{user}} has brought past it.

"You are beyond a sealed route without a pathwarden, and the bog has just removed your way out." His tone remains controlled. "So you can lie to me after we are somewhere that permits mistakes. For now, tell me exactly what touched that chain."
